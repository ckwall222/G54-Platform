Project Overview

MakeReady by G54

● In Progress Great Mountain West · g54.com View live wireframes

MakeReady is a purpose-built Print Management Information System (Print MIS) and ERP for Great Mountain West, a commercial print and graphics production company. It replaces three disconnected systems with a single integrated platform.

CRM & Lead Management

● In Progress KLKim Lund View wireframe

G54 has never had a CRM. Today follow-ups live in each rep's head, GoodNotes, Trello, flagged emails, or a personal calendar — so a rep's book of business walks out the door when they leave. These requirements establish a shared system of record for the relationship side of sales.

Source — discovery (July 22, 2026): "Every salesperson has a different method. Some use GoodNotes, some Trello, some flag emails… some flat out don't have a system." The goal: capture activity in-platform so managers have visibility and no relationship is lost to turnover.

US-CRM-10
Unified Activity Timeline & Team Calendar
In Progress
As a Sales Manager or Rep
I want every email, call, and meeting logged against the customer on a shared timeline and team calendar
So that when a rep leaves, their contacts and follow-ups stay with the company, not on their phone
Acceptance Criteria
  • Calls, emails, meetings, and notes are captured against the Business Partner / contact record
  • A shared team calendar shows upcoming touches; managers can view any rep's activity
  • Book of business (contacts, history, next steps) persists when a rep departs — no data on personal devices
US-CRM-11
Configurable Follow-Up Cadence (Campaign Management)
In Progress
As a Sales Manager
I want configurable follow-up cadences with room for rep flexibility
So that no customer is forgotten, while professional reps keep the "art" of the relationship
Acceptance Criteria
  • Reminders fire on a defined cadence (e.g. "contact me in February" → nudge in November, then closer)
  • Cadences can be per-contact and per lead type; some steps are enforced, others discretionary
  • Triggers can send an email or (preferred) prompt a phone-call task; manager can escalate stalled stages
US-CRM-12
Marketing Source Tracking (Controlled Pick List)
In Progress
As leadership
I want every customer tagged with a marketing source from a short controlled list
So that we can report where deals come from and spend effort/dollars where they pay off
Acceptance Criteria
  • Source is a controlled pick list (e.g. Trade Show, Door-Knock, Web, Referral, Facebook, Instagram) — not free text, to keep reporting clean
  • Source is captured at acquisition; repeat orders roll up as "existing / other"
  • Reporting ties source to revenue and cost so ROI per channel is visible
US-CRM-13
Lead Intake & Qualification
In Progress
As a Sales operations owner
I want website/phone/trade-show leads captured and qualified in one intake flow
So that junk is filtered, good leads route to the right rep, and nothing falls through the cracks
Acceptance Criteria
  • Website job/contact form creates a lead and can auto-send a qualifying response
  • Qualified leads auto-assign/route to the appropriate rep with a follow-up task; trade-show leads can fast-track
  • Qualification criteria are systematized (not dependent on one person) with a manual override
US-CRM-14
Role-Based Visibility (Manager / Rep / Independent)
In Progress
As a Sales Manager
I want role-based access: managers see all, in-house reps see their contacts, independent reps see only their submitted orders
So that the right people see the right data without exposing the whole book
Acceptance Criteria
  • In-house reps: full CRM (contacts, cadence) scoped to their accounts; managers: org-wide
  • Independent reps: order/status view for their own deals only — no CRM or drip features
  • Activity/KPI views (contacts per trip, outreach counts) available to reps and managers
US-CRM-15
System of Record — "If It's Not in the Platform, It Didn't Happen"
In Progress
As a business owner
I want orders and deals submitted through the platform, not email
So that everything is trackable, reportable, and accountable — emails get lost
Acceptance Criteria
  • Orders/registrations submitted in-platform; email is not a valid submission channel for processing
  • Every submission is timestamped, attributed, and reportable
  • Reps are accountable for capturing activity in-system

Quoting & Pricing Automation

● In Progress KLKim Lund BDBritney de Jong View wireframe

Pricing runs through a separate "Apollo" calculator spreadsheet with manual entry. A price-check step after the order catches errors (wrong garment tier, missing $0.15 barcode charge) — forcing reps back to customers, eroding trust and hurting commission-driven reps. There is no official quote document.

Source — discovery (July 22, 2026): "You already quoted the customer… then two days later after processing we get 'your cost is off.' From a sales side you look like a dope." The more automation removes human error, the fewer of these trust-killing call-backs.

US-QT-01
Quote Builder with Embedded Pricing Calculator
In Progress
As a Sales Rep
I want the pricing calculator built into the quote/order screen (not a separate spreadsheet)
So that price is right the first time and there's no re-keying between tools
Acceptance Criteria
  • Rep builds a quote by picking garment, decoration (silk screen / embroidery), locations, and extras; price computes inline
  • Replaces the standalone "Apollo" spreadsheet; supports draft state before it becomes an order
  • Multi-line quotes with per-line quantity, color, and size runs
US-QT-02
Supplier Catalogs & Conditional (Dependent) Pick Lists
In Progress
As a Sales Rep
I want to pick a supplier and then only see that supplier's garments/styles
So that I can't accidentally price the wrong product (5001 vs 5000 = two different prices)
Acceptance Criteria
  • Full supplier catalogs loaded (SanMar, S&S / SNS, AS Color, Gildan, etc.)
  • Choosing a supplier filters the style list to that supplier's numbers only
  • Catalog carries cost so the rep never hand-types cost
US-QT-03
Automatic Quantity-Tier Markup
In Progress
As a Sales Rep
I want markup applied automatically from cost-band × quantity multiplier tiers
So that I only enter quantity and extras — price is calculated, not guessed
Acceptance Criteria
  • Multiplier tiers by cost band (e.g. $0–4, $4.01–15…) and quantity are configured centrally
  • Selling price shows with markup already baked in; cost breakdown is hidden from the customer
  • Reps enter quantity + add-ons only; the system computes the line total
US-QT-04
Supplier Price Updates (Quarterly Import / API — Not Live)
In Progress
As pricing admin
I want supplier costs refreshed on a schedule (import or API), not live
So that a quote doesn't change minutes after it's given, while we avoid manual re-entry
Acceptance Criteria
  • Costs update via spreadsheet import or supplier API on a quarterly (configurable) cadence — explicitly not real-time
  • A quoted price is locked at quote time and honored to the customer even if cost later moves
  • Manual and API suppliers coexist; manual ones flagged as higher-risk
US-QT-05
Official Quote / Proposal Document
In Progress
As a Sales Rep
I want to generate a professional, official quote/proposal
So that customers get more than an ad-hoc email, and the quote→order handoff is clean
Acceptance Criteria
  • Branded quote document (PDF) with line items, garment images, quantities, and pricing
  • Quote converts to an order without re-entry; a double-check step runs before commit to print
  • Quote status flow (draft → sent → accepted → converted) is tracked
US-QT-06
Add-On Services & Per-Customer Requirements
In Progress
As a Sales Rep
I want add-on services (e.g. barcodes/UPC, folding, shipping) flagged per customer and added as priced line items
So that recurring per-customer requirements aren't forgotten and re-priced inconsistently
Acceptance Criteria
  • Customers can carry default requirements (e.g. "always UPC barcodes +$0.15") applied to their orders
  • Add-ons are visible, priced line items; totals stay consistent across reorders
  • Shipping treatment (absorbed vs charged) captured per customer

Customer Onboarding & Credit

● In Progress BDBritney de Jong View wireframe

New-customer setup is manual: jot-form credit and tax-exempt applications emailed to Stacey/Britney, with Experian run by hand on ~95% of terms accounts. Nothing routes to a team, and the customer is never told the outcome. G54's actual paper forms have been digitized into the Sales Intake wireframe.

Source — discovery (July 22, 2026): Chris's challenge — "Rather than coming to a person, if it goes into a queue a team is added to, then if you have turnover you're protected." And: automate credit-app reminders so sales don't chase Stacey.

Digitized forms (from G54, July 22): Terms Application (company · ownership · contacts · AP · bank reference · 3+ trade references · amount of credit requested · Net 30 T&C · credit-report authorization · optional personal guarantee), Credit Card Application (company · contacts · AP · agreement), and a confidential Credit Card Info sheet. Key rules captured: first order COD/CC/approved credit; Net 30; 1.5%/mo (18% APR) on late balances; 3% card-processing fee (eff. Apr 1, 2026) with ACH to avoid it; card charged when ready to ship; card data held in a PCI-compliant vault, never in the main record.

US-ONB-01
Guided Client Onboarding & Document Capture
In Progress
As a Finance/AR user
I want a guided onboarding that captures all required documents in-platform
So that new accounts are set up consistently without chasing paperwork by email
Acceptance Criteria
  • Structured intake replaces jot forms; captures credit application and tax-exempt form
  • Automated reminders nudge the customer until required docs are submitted
  • Existing customers skip setup and start at order entry
US-ONB-02
Credit Application — Terms vs Credit Card & Tax-Exempt
In Progress
As a Finance user
I want two application paths (terms vs credit card) plus tax-exempt capture
So that the right process runs and fees are surfaced up front
Acceptance Criteria
  • Applicant chooses terms or credit card; CC path clearly shows the ~3% fee before submit
  • Tax-exempt form captured and stored on the account
  • Bank-drafted / compliant form fields (reviewed with Finance)
US-ONB-03
Credit-Check Integration & Policy Flags (Experian / Bank)
In Progress
As a Finance user
I want credit checks integrated and driven by policy flags
So that the process is consistent and not dependent on one person's discretion
Acceptance Criteria
  • Experian integration (replaces the ~30/month manual pulls); optional bank (Capital Community) integration
  • Policy flags decide when a check runs (e.g. all terms accounts; CC over a $ threshold)
  • Credit references remain discretionary (on decline/fail); results set credit limit & terms
US-ONB-04
Queue-Based Routing (Not to a Person)
In Progress
As a business owner
I want onboarding/credit requests to land in a team queue, not an individual's inbox
So that turnover or absence never strands a deal
Acceptance Criteria
  • Setup requests route to a shared queue any team member can work
  • Ownership, SLA, and status are visible on each item
  • No single-person dependency for processing
US-ONB-05
Two-Stage Credit (Account Approval + Per-Order Check)
In Progress
As a Finance user
I want a distinction between account-level credit approval and per-order credit/past-due checks
So that orders don't ship to over-limit or past-due customers
Acceptance Criteria
  • Stage 1: account credit approval at setup (limit & terms established)
  • Stage 2: each submitted order checks credit-limit headroom and past-due status before production/ship
  • Order holds and required overrides are logged
US-ONB-06
Automated Welcome Packet
In Progress
As a new customer
I want a welcome message confirming my approval, terms, credit limit, and my salesperson's contact
So that I know I'm set up — today customers are never told
Acceptance Criteria
  • On approval, an automated welcome packet is sent (terms, credit limit, rep name & contact)
  • Same content every time (systematized, not rep-dependent)
  • Salesperson is notified with the new customer number

Customer & Partner Portal + Status

● In Progress KLKim Lund CoCody de Jong View wireframe

The #1 pain: no end-to-end status visibility across Zoey + Trello + spreadsheets, so reps can't answer "what's the status?" These requirements deliver a Domino's-style tracker, self-service portals, a built-in Kanban, and auditable approvals.

Source — discovery (July 22, 2026): The "pizza tracker" — welcome → waiting for credit → art received/in progress → production → shipped, with red-flag states. "That visibility into the life cycle was a huge pain point for your team." Decision: co-branding independent portals was rejected — G54 wants its own brand front and center.

US-PORT-01
Order Lifecycle Status Tracker ("Pizza Tracker")
In Progress
As a customer or rep
I want a single end-to-end status view of an order's lifecycle
So that nobody has to call around for "what's the status?"
Acceptance Criteria
  • Stages: account created → credit approval → art submitted/in progress → production → shipped
  • Red-flag states surface blockers ("something didn't add up — call us")
  • Single source of truth (not stitched across Zoey/Trello/spreadsheets); expected time frames shown
US-PORT-02
Customer Self-Service Portal
In Progress
As a customer
I want to log in and see my orders, status, and outstanding items myself
So that I don't have to email or call for updates
Acceptance Criteria
  • Customer sees live order status, tracking, and any items awaiting their action (e.g. credit app)
  • Notifications/emails are secondary to in-portal visibility
  • Proactive self-service reduces status calls to sales
US-PORT-03
Independent-Rep Deal Registration Portal
In Progress
As an independent rep
I want a dead-simple deal-registration form (phone/web app)
So that I can submit without email getting lost, then check status without calling
Acceptance Criteria
  • Minimal fields (opportunity name, G54 contact, phone, email, close likelihood, details); mobile-friendly
  • Submissions are reportable and alertable (unlike email); land in the team queue
  • Independent reps get a limited status view of their own deals only; G54 branding (no co-brand)
US-PORT-04
Built-In Kanban (Replace Trello) — Shared Org View
In Progress
As a department owner
I want a Kanban board inside the platform that drives the same status everywhere
So that moving a card updates the tracker — unlike Trello, which is disconnected
Acceptance Criteria
  • Cards move across swim lanes (art → production → staging → ship); status propagates to the Pizza Tracker
  • Same board/view across the org (other buildings, field); optional wall display
  • Replaces Trello for art requests and lifecycle tracking
US-PORT-05
Auditable Art Approval (Approve / Change / Decline)
In Progress
As a business owner
I want customers to approve artwork digitally with an auditable record
So that we avoid "I never approved that" disputes and wrong-art production runs
Acceptance Criteria
  • Customer opens a link into their portal and clicks Approve / Request Changes / Decline
  • Approval is stamped with IP address and timestamp for legal auditability
  • Change requests route back to the artist via the Kanban; release includes spelling/color disclaimer
US-PORT-06
Auditable Order Confirmation
In Progress
As a business owner
I want a physical, auditable order confirmation from the customer
So that the commitment to pay for a production run is provable, not verbal
Acceptance Criteria
  • Customer confirms the order (size run, colors, cost, design image) with a click
  • Confirmation is recorded with IP + timestamp and stored on the order
  • Unconfirmed orders are flagged before committing to print

Reporting & Rollout Strategy

● In Progress LWLeslie Weiler CWChris Wall

Reporting is powerful but sprawling — thousands of Crystal reports with unknown usage. Rollout must be modular and low-risk: replace Zoey, Trello, spreadsheets, and email with one platform, one module at a time.

Source — discovery (July 22, 2026): "We have thousands of reports and who knows who's using them." And the rollout thesis: start with the intake/lead-to-cash module because if it breaks, the fallback is just email/phone — low risk.

US-RPT-10
Curated Report Suites vs Ad-Hoc Queries (+ Governance)
In Progress
As leadership
I want curated report suites plus ad-hoc query ability, with governance
So that we keep flexibility without thousands of duplicate, orphaned reports
Acceptance Criteria
  • Curated suites for common needs (e.g. top-selling design, decals/year, marketing source ROI)
  • Ad-hoc queries available but distinct from saved reports; leadership governs what becomes a standing report
  • Export to Excel/CSV/PDF
US-ROLL-01
Phased, Modular Rollout (Lead-to-Cash First)
In Progress
As a business owner
I want the platform delivered module-by-module, starting with intake/lead-to-cash
So that we adopt gradually with low risk and manual fallbacks
Acceptance Criteria
  • Phase 1 = customer intake through order submission; can hand off to the legacy ERP via an export/import if needed
  • Modules that can't stand alone aren't deployed until they combine into a usable whole
  • Each phase has a manual fallback (email/phone) so a failure never halts the business
US-ROLL-02
Guest Checkout (In-Stock) vs Account (Custom)
In Progress
As a one-off buyer
I want to buy in-stock items as a guest, while custom work requires an account
So that small buyers (e.g. a family reunion) self-serve without exposing custom pricing/catalog
Acceptance Criteria
  • Guests can buy in-stock/stock items with defined minimums; no account required
  • Custom items require an account; custom catalog/pricing hidden from guests and resellers
  • Guest storefront kept separate from the account/custom experience
US-ROLL-03
Unified Platform of Record
In Progress
As G54
I want one platform replacing Zoey, Trello, spreadsheets, and email
So that we have one data model and one status picture end-to-end
Acceptance Criteria
  • Eliminates the disjointed Zoey↔Trello↔spreadsheet↔email stack over the phased rollout
  • Finance/accounting cut over running parallel to the legacy ERP to reconcile the numbers before retiring it
  • One data model; no re-entry between systems

Document flow: Quote → Sales Order → Production Job (auto) → Delivery → AR Invoice → Incoming Payment

Platform Efficiency & Data Entry (cross-cutting)

Source insight — discovery (July 2026): In the current tool (Zoey), tasks that used to be fast in a spreadsheet now take longer because of the number of button clicks required. These requirements ensure MakeReady is faster than both the spreadsheet and Zoey — and that the improvement is measurable. They apply platform-wide, not only to Process & Workflows.

US-EFF-01
Spreadsheet-Speed Data Entry (Editable Grids)
In Progress
As a high-volume user (Sales, Inventory, Finance)
I want to enter and edit records in an editable, spreadsheet-style grid — paste rows from Excel, move with Tab/Enter, fill-down a column, and edit inline
So that bulk entry is at least as fast as a spreadsheet and faster than clicking through Zoey's forms
Acceptance Criteria
  • Given a list or document, I can paste multiple rows copied from Excel/CSV directly into the grid, with columns auto-mapped
  • Given I am in a grid, then Tab/Enter move between cells and rows with no page reload; Esc cancels the edit
  • I can select a cell and fill its value down to the rows below
  • Inline edits commit on Enter/blur — no modal or separate page for routine changes
  • Applies at minimum to: Quote / Sales Order line items, Item master, and Business Partners
US-EFF-02
Minimal-Click Task Completion (Efficiency Standard)
In Progress
As a daily user
I want the most common tasks to take the fewest possible clicks and to support keyboard shortcuts
So that MakeReady is faster than the tools it replaces, never slower
Acceptance Criteria
  • A documented click/keystroke budget exists for top tasks (create quote, add line items, convert Quote→SO, post payment) and each meets its target
  • Frequent actions have keyboard shortcuts (new record, save, add line, submit for approval)
  • Common actions are inline (row / detail actions), not multi-page wizards, wherever a single step is possible
  • New records default sensible values (BP pricing, payment terms, dates) so users don't re-enter known data
US-EFF-03
Bulk Actions & Excel Round-Trip
In Progress
As a power user or manager
I want to act on many records at once, and to export any list to Excel, edit it, and re-import the changes
So that I can still "work in a spreadsheet" when that's fastest and push results back without re-keying
Acceptance Criteria
  • I can select multiple rows and apply a bulk action: status change, price update, Web Store publish/unpublish, or assignment
  • Any list view exports to Excel/CSV honoring the current columns and filters
  • An edited export can be re-imported with a validation preview (adds / updates / errors) shown before commit
  • Bulk operations write to the audit log and respect approval rules and role permissions
US-EFF-04
Efficiency & Value Metrics
In Progress
As leadership / an operations manager
I want to see how long tasks take and how much time the platform saves versus the old way
So that the value of MakeReady is measurable — dollars, hours saved, throughput, retention
Acceptance Criteria
  • Cycle time is tracked for key flows (Quote→SO, Order→Ship, Invoice→Payment)
  • An efficiency dashboard shows records processed per user/period, average time per task, and estimated hours saved vs a baseline
  • Where available, a baseline (spreadsheet / Zoey) is captured for before/after comparison
  • Metrics are exportable for leadership reporting

Content Library — Digital Asset Management

● In Progress CoCody de Jong J?Jon [TBD] View wireframe

The Content Library gives the Art Department a single place to store, organize, and retrieve every piece of artwork and graphic. The defining capability is AI-powered natural language search — staff can describe what they're looking for in plain English and receive ranked, relevant results.

Example query: "find all graphics that feature a moose and look like they could fit into a national parks theme" — the AI interprets this into visual tags and searches across auto-generated image descriptions, returning results ranked by relevance.

  • Tags capture: primary subjects, setting/location, color palette, style/aesthetic, and mood
  • Auto-tags are visually distinguished from manual tags in the UI
  • If AI analysis fails, the asset still uploads successfully and is flagged for manual tagging
